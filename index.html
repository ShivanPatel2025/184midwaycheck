Project Summary

In the first half of our Chladni Plate Simulation project, we've successfully created our framework and are able to simulate multiple frequencies and the patterns that form from them. We've developed our simulation by incorporating a model of the plate's vibrations using wave functions and the Chladni equation. Our simulated plate is divided into a grid, and by applying the Chladni equation at each of these points, we can calculate the vibration intensity, which corresponds to the amplitude of the wave at that particular location. Our model on the movement of particles consists of us calculating the gradient of the vibration intensity at each grid point. From this, we can determine the direction of the steepest descent from areas of high intensity to low intensity. This gradient effectively shows how particles are drawn towards the nodal lines, or areas of zero amplitude. With each particle's position being updated based on the gradiant, our simulation is able to show the formation of distinct patterns at different frequencies and how particles move and settle into these patterns over time.
